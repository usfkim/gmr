Medical Doctors Licensing

The Uganda Medical Council (UMC) is responsible for regulating the practice of medicine, dentistry, and physician assistantship in Uganda. All medical practitioners must be registered and licensed by the UMC to legally practice in Uganda.

Licensing Requirements

To qualify for medical licensing in Uganda, applicants must have:

  • A medical degree (MBChB, MBBS, MD) from a recognized medical school
  • Completed a mandatory housemanship (internship) program at an approved health facility in Uganda
  • For foreign-trained doctors: Verification of medical credentials and passing the MDC's registration examination
  • For specialists: Completion of postgraduate training and certification from recognized institutions

The following documents are required for medical licensing:

  • Original medical degree certificate and academic transcripts
  • Certificate of completion of housemanship/internship
  • Certificate of good standing from previous licensing authority (for foreign-trained doctors)
  • Valid identification (National ID, Passport)
  • Proof of Ugandan citizenship or work permit for non-Ugandans
  • Recent passport-sized photographs
  • Certificate of registration with the Uganda Medical Association (recommended)

Professional qualifications required for different categories:

General Practitioners

  • Basic medical degree and completed housemanship
  • Passing the MDC's registration examination (if applicable)

Specialists

  • Fellowship or membership of Uganda College of Physicians and Surgeons
  • OR equivalent qualification from recognized international bodies (e.g., FRCPS, FRCS, American Board certification)
  • Minimum of 3-7 years of postgraduate training depending on specialty

Foreign-Trained Doctors

  • Verification of credentials through Primary Source Verification
  • Passing the MDC's registration examination
  • Completion of 6-12 months of supervised clinical practice in Uganda

The registration process involves several stages:

Provisional Registration

  • Required for new graduates to undertake housemanship
  • Valid for the duration of housemanship (typically 2 years)
  • Limited to practice under supervision in approved institutions

Temporary Registration

  • For foreign doctors coming to Uganda for short-term work
  • Valid for up to 3 months (renewable)
  • Limited to specific institution and purpose

Full Registration

  • Granted after successful completion of housemanship
  • Allows independent practice within scope of training
  • Requires annual renewal

Specialist Registration

  • Additional registration for qualified specialists
  • Requires proof of specialist qualification
  • Listed in the specialist register

Application Process

1

Online Application Submission

Complete and submit the online application form through the MDC portal.

  • Create an account on the MDC online portal
  • Fill out the application form with personal and professional details
  • Upload scanned copies of required documents
  • Pay the application processing fee online

Estimated Time: 1-2 hours

2

Document Verification

The MDC verifies all submitted documents and credentials.

  • MDC reviews all submitted documents
  • Primary source verification of medical degree and credentials
  • Verification of housemanship completion
  • Background checks for professional misconduct

Estimated Time: 2-4 weeks

3

Registration Examination (if applicable)

Foreign-trained doctors must pass the MDC registration examination.

  • Schedule examination date through the MDC portal
  • Pay examination fee
  • Complete written examination
  • Complete clinical skills assessment

Estimated Time: 1-2 days for exam, 2-3 weeks for results

4

Interview and Oath Taking

Successful applicants attend an interview and take the medical oath.

  • Schedule interview appointment
  • Attend in-person interview with the MDC board
  • Take the Hippocratic Oath or Physician's Pledge
  • Receive orientation on professional ethics and regulations

Estimated Time: 1 day

5

License Issuance

Pay registration fee and receive your medical license.

  • Pay the registration fee
  • Receive license certificate and registration number
  • Obtain practitioner ID card
  • Entry into the Uganda Medical Registry database

Estimated Time: 1-2 weeks

Overall Process Timeline

Estimated Total Time: 4-8 weeks

Important Note

Processing times may vary based on application volume, document verification complexity, and examination schedules. Foreign-trained doctors typically experience longer processing times due to additional verification requirements.

License Renewal

Medical licenses in Uganda must be renewed annually. The renewal process ensures that practitioners maintain their professional standards and continue their professional development.

Renewal Requirements

  • Renewal application submitted at least 30 days before expiration

  • Payment of annual renewal fee

  • Proof of completed Continuing Professional Development (CPD) credits

  • Updated professional information (if applicable)

  • Declaration of good standing and fitness to practice

Continuing Professional Development

All licensed medical practitioners must complete a minimum number of CPD credits annually:

General Practitioners

Minimum 30 CPD credits per year

Specialists

Minimum 40 CPD credits per year

Approved CPD Activities

  • • Conferences and workshops
  • • Medical journal publications
  • • Online medical courses
  • • Clinical audits and research
  • • Teaching and mentoring activities

Renewal Process

  1. 1

    Log in to the MDC online portal using your registration credentials

  2. 2

    Complete the renewal application form and update any personal or professional information

  3. 3

    Upload evidence of completed CPD activities and credits

  4. 4

    Pay the renewal fee through the online payment system

  5. 5

    Receive digital confirmation of renewal and updated license certificate

Late Renewal Warning

Failure to renew your license before the expiration date will result in a late fee. Practicing with an expired license is illegal and may result in disciplinary action. Licenses expired for more than 3 months may require additional reinstatement procedures.

If you fail to complete the required CPD credits, your license renewal may be denied or conditionally approved with a remediation plan. For first-time failures, you may be granted a conditional renewal with a requirement to complete the missing credits plus additional credits within a specified timeframe (usually 3-6 months). Repeated failure to meet CPD requirements may result in suspension of your license until the requirements are met.

Foreign medical qualifications must undergo Primary Source Verification (PSV) through the MDC's approved verification service. This involves direct verification with the issuing institutions of your medical degree, postgraduate training, and previous licensing authorities. You will need to submit your credentials to the verification service, pay the verification fee, and authorize the release of information. The verification process typically takes 4-8 weeks to complete.

Provisional registration is granted to new medical graduates to undertake their housemanship (internship) training. It restricts practice to approved training institutions under supervision and is valid only for the duration of the housemanship (typically 2 years). Full registration is granted after successful completion of housemanship and allows independent practice within the scope of training. Full registration must be renewed annually but does not have the same practice restrictions as provisional registration.
